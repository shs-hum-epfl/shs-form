Colour Experience Study — Emotional Perception of Colours

Experimental Cognitive Psychology · HUM 403 · EPFL / UNIL · 2025
You will see 80 colour patches one at a time. For each, rate how the colour feels to you on a scale from 1 to 7. The survey takes about 20 minutes.

Consent & data protection

Look around at the room you're in. Have you ever wondered about the colours of your walls, furniture, other objects? Do you think they impact how you feel?

For more than 10 years, our research group has studied how people perceive and associate colours. This time, we aim to replicate and extend Song et al. (2025; BMC Psychology, 13:650) on the emotional valence of colours across university students from art programmes and from other fields.

If you agree to take part, you should spare about 20 minutes of your time. Please complete the study in a quiet place. If possible, do not use your smartphone, because the colour patches may render differently on small displays.

Data use. We will use the data for scientific purposes only, evaluated and published only in aggregated form. It will be impossible to identify individual participants in the data, publications, or presentations.

Voluntary participation. Your participation is voluntary. You can stop at any time without giving reasons and without consequences. If the study is terminated prematurely, the data collected up to that point will be deleted.

By completing this survey, you agree that we can use your anonymous data for research.

How the rating task works

You will now see 80 coloured patches, one at a time. Each patch appears on its own page.

What you have to do

For each colour, rate its emotional valence on a scale from 1 to 7.

What is "emotional valence"?

In plain words: how positive or negative does the colour feel to you?

  • Positive feelings = pleasant, cheerful, uplifting, comforting, inviting.
  • Negative feelings = unpleasant, gloomy, irritating, oppressive, uncomfortable.
  • Neutral = you feel nothing particular either way.

Don't rate whether the colour is pretty or whether you like it as a colour. Rate the feeling it evokes in you right now.

1 = very negative · 4 = neutral · 7 = very positive

A few things to remember

  • There are no right or wrong answers.
  • Give your first impression. Don't overthink.
  • Don't compare each colour to the one shown before. Judge each one on its own.
  • Please complete the study in one continuous session.
